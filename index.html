About Me

A recent graduate with a B.Sc. (Hons) in Mechanical Engineering (Specialization in Mechatronics), I have a strong foundation in robotics and mechanical systems. I am passionate about applying my skills in designing, developing, and testing engineering systems to tackle complex challenges.

Research Focus

My research interests are primarily at the intersection of bio-inspired locomotion and advanced control systems. I am particularly fascinated by the idea of translating principles from nature into novel robotic mechanisms and control strategies. I believe that a single, special observation of animal behavior can lead to elegant, significant improvements in robot function.

In parallel with this, I have a deep passion for complex control challenges. I enjoy working with mathematical and analytical methods to develop algorithms that allow robots to operate effectively in uncertain or unstructured environments.

Long-Term Goals

Ultimately, my long-term goal is to explore swarm robotics, inspired by the collective intelligence of bees and ants. I am deeply compelled by the idea of translating these social and distributed strategies into engineered multi-robot systems, where simple individual rules produce robust and scalable group behavior.
